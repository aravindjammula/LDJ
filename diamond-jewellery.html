Understanding Diamond Quality

The 4CsCut, Color, Clarity, and Caratare the universal standards for evaluating diamond quality. Learn how these factors determine a diamond's beauty and value.

Cut

The most important factor affecting a diamond's brilliance. A well-cut diamond reflects light beautifully, creating exceptional sparkle and fire.

Scale: Excellent to Poor Our Standard: Very Good to Excellent

Color

Measures a diamond's lack of color. Truly colorless diamonds are rare and highly valued, while subtle tints can affect value.

Scale: D (Colorless) to Z Our Range: D to J

Clarity

Refers to the absence of inclusions and blemishes. Fewer imperfections mean higher clarity and greater value.

Scale: Flawless to Included Our Range: IF to SI2

Carat

The unit of weight for diamonds. Larger diamonds are rarer, but cut, color, and clarity also significantly impact beauty and value.

Unit: Carats (ct) Our Range: 0.25ct to 10+ ct

Our Certification Standards

Every diamond in our collection over 0.50 carats comes with certification from internationally recognized gemological laboratories. These documents provide an unbiased assessment of the diamond's characteristics.

We work primarily with GIA (Gemological Institute of America) and IGI (International Gemological Institute), the most respected names in diamond grading. Each certificate includes a detailed analysis of the 4Cs, along with diagrams showing inclusions and proportions.

Independent Verification

Third-party certification ensures objective, unbiased grading of every diamond's quality characteristics.

Full Disclosure

Complete transparency about diamond characteristics, treatments, and enhancements if applicable.

Security Features

Certificates include security holograms and unique identification numbers to prevent fraud.
